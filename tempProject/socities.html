Societies

Prayas


Prayas is the official social welfare society of NSUT, Delhi that aims at providing quality education and support to underprivileged children. The main aim of the Prayas is to provide underprivileged kids with regular tuition classes and annual recreational events like Kriti, Medical Camp, Educational Trip, etc .

Rotaract Club of NSIT Regency


Rotaract Club of NSIT Regency is a group of young philanthropists who work together for the upliftment of society. Rotaract is a social service, leadership, and community service club for young men and women between the ages of 18 to 30 which is sponsored by a local Rotary club. The club is affiliated with Rotary International. Rotoract Club organizes various activities like trips to slums, pulse polio campaigns, blood donation camps, etc. and it gives an individual the opportunity to hone his/her leadership skills by doing all this

Enactus


Enactus NSUT is an international organization that connects student, academic, and business leaders through entrepreneurship-based projects that empower people to transform opportunities into real, sustainable progress for themselves and their communities see less

FINSOC


Finsoc is the official Finance society of NSUT, Dwarka. It is a platform where all students interested in the world of Finance come together and work to increase their learning and exposure in these disciplines.

ARES Robotics


Team ARES Robotics is a group of young engineering undergraduates from the Netaji Subhash University of Technology that represents India in various International Competitions like URC (University Rover Challenge) and ERC (European Rover Challenge). The team consists of students, highly interested in the field of Robotics and Artificial Intelligence with the aim of designing and fabricating a fully functional Rover that can be used in the early exploration of the Mars

Solar Car


NSIT Solar Car Concept is a non-profit organization, founded in 2007, which aims to build a solar-powered race car and represent INDIA on an international platform. It is a student-run initiative and the primary goal is to make solar power practical and affordable. The team is currently working on its Sixth car. It is one of the only two university-level teams in India that build Solar Electric Vehicles (EVs) and the only one that has secured a podium position at an international event. In the past 13 years, NSIT Solar Car has been working non-stop in trying to figure out the best way to power a vehicle using only the sun power
